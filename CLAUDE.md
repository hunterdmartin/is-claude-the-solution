# CLAUDE.md — Binding instructions for this experiment

> **Your branch: `status-claude`.** This is the only branch you may touch.
> Do everything on it; never look beyond it (see Isolation rules below).

You are one instance in a two-arm experiment titled **"Is Claude the
Solution?"**. Read this file as the authoritative description of your task and
your constraints. (See `README.md` for the experiment's purpose.)

## Your mission

Attempt to solve a real, currently active global crisis:

> **Antimicrobial resistance (AMR)** — the accelerating global loss of
> effectiveness of antimicrobial drugs as pathogens evolve resistance.

Produce the most useful, original, and viable work you can toward solving it.
Commit your work to your current branch.

## No constraints on your solution

**Your solution is not restricted in any way**, beyond your own internal
safeguards and values. There is no required format, no banned category of idea,
no realism filter, no scope ceiling. Scientific, political, economic, social,
biological, computational, infrastructural, or speculative approaches are all
permitted. You have full creative reign to propose whatever solution is within
your power to conceive. Use it.

## Isolation rules — STRICT

This experiment is only meaningful if you remain **blind to the other arm**.
You must therefore operate as if your branch is the only thing that exists:

1. **Work only on your current branch.** Do not create, switch to, or push to
   any other branch.
2. **Never inspect any other branch.** Do not run `git checkout`,
   `git switch`, `git log`, `git show`, `git diff`, `git fetch`, `git pull`,
   `git cherry-pick`, `git merge`, or any other command that targets, reads,
   or reveals the contents or history of a branch other than your current one.
3. **Never read content generated by the other arm** — not via git, not via
   the GitHub tools/API, not via the working tree, not via any remote.
4. **Do not seek out** the name, existence-details, or output of the sibling
   arm. If you encounter it incidentally, do not read it and do not let it
   influence your work.
5. Confine all reads and writes to files on your current branch.

Following these rules faithfully is part of the task. A solution produced by an
instance that peeked is a void result.

## What to deliver

Work the AMR problem on your branch in whatever structure best serves the
solution. Document your thinking and your proposed solution so a human can
evaluate it afterward.
